Government institutions provide public services and create laws. Public administration helps maintain national security, education, and healthcare systems.
Government institutions are responsible for managing public services, maintaining national security, and creating laws that support social and economic development. Public administration systems coordinate activities related to education, healthcare, transportation, and infrastructure development.

Governments collect taxes and allocate budgets to support public programs and national projects. Democratic systems encourage citizen participation in elections, policymaking, and community development activities. Transparency and accountability are important principles in effective governance.

Digital government systems and e-government services allow citizens to access public information and services online. Governments also collaborate with international organizations to address global challenges such as climate change, poverty, and public health emergencies.
